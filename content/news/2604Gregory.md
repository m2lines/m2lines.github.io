**Will Gregory** et al. introduce **[FloeNet](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2603.12449), a machine-learning emulator trained on the GFDL global sea ice model (SIS2)** to reproduce key sea-ice and snow-on-sea-ice processes while conserving mass. The model emulates 6-hour tendencies related to ice and snow growth, melt, and advection. Trained on reanalysis-forced simulations, FloeNet was tested across different climate states, including pre-industrial and an increasing CO₂ scenario. It accurately **reproduces sea-ice mean state, trends, and interannual variability, outperforming non-conservative approaches.** FloeNet also captures the balance between thermodynamic and dynamic responses to forcing and reproduces coupling-related variables such as ice-surface temperature and ocean salt fluxes. These results suggest strong potential for improving the representation of polar processes in climate emulators.
